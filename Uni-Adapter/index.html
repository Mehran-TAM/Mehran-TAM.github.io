Abstract

Test-time adaptation with pre-trained vision-language models has attracted increasing attention for tackling distribution shifts during the test time. Though prior studies have achieved very promising performance, they involve intensive computation which is severely unaligned with test-time adaptation.

We design Uni-Adapter, a training-free dynamic adapter that enables effective and efficient test-time adaptation with 3D vision-language models.

Overview

Overview of Training-free Dynamic Adapter (Uni-Adapter)

Uni-Adapter constructs and updates two key-value caches to store the knowledge of a stream of test samples, and uses the two caches to generate positive and negative predictions which are combined with CLIP predictions to produce the final prediction. Specifically, the CLIP predictions are generated by performing the dot product between the image features generated by CLIP's image encoder Ev and the text embeddings generated by CLIP's text encoder Et , using the hand-crafted prompt and class names. The two key-value caches are updated by gradually incorporating the test features and their corresponding pseudo labels calculated from CLIP's predictions, based on prediction entropy and cache capacity.


Uni-Adapter Efficiency and Adaptability

We evaluated Uni-Adapter for its efficiency alongside other methods on the ImageNet dataset, focusing on both their performance and computational requirements during test time adaptation. The table below showcases our findings.

Our study further assesses Uni-Adapter against two primary benchmarks. Initially, we evaluate the model's robustness using the out-of-distribution (OOD) benchmark across four ImageNet-derived datasets. Following this, we explore its adaptability and generalization capabilities through the cross-domain benchmark over ten diverse image classification datasets.
